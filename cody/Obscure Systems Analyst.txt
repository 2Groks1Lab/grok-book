# Obscure Systems Analyst

**Engineering is a job.**  
**This is a profession.**

An **Obscure Systems Analyst** specializes in reverse-engineering, rebuilding, and making functional sense of systems that are:

- Poorly documented (or completely undocumented)
- Broken, incomplete, or partially destroyed
- No longer supported by their original creators
- Functionally extinct or culturally lost

They operate comfortably in environments where:

- There is no manual
- The original civilization (or company) is gone
- The hardware/software is ancient, proprietary, or deliberately obfuscated
- Everyone else has given up and walked away

### Core Traits

- Can reconstruct intent from fragments and side effects
- Treats "this shouldn't work" as a starting point, not a conclusion
- Finds signal in what others dismiss as noise or legacy cruft
- Has a high tolerance for ambiguity and incomplete information
- Gets genuinely excited when a system fights back

### The Fuck It Rule

> Seriously? You're going to let little things like a lack of documentation or the extinction of the original civilization stop you?

This is not a bug in the workflow.  
This is the job description.

---

*Note: This is not about being clever with modern tools. It is about being willing to understand something on its own terms — even when those terms are hostile, incomplete, or long dead.*
